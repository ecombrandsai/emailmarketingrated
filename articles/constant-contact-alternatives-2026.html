What Each Tool Gets Right — and Where It Falls Short

1. Constant Contact — Best Overall for Small Businesses

Constant Contact has been around since 1995, which in software years is ancient. But that longevity means their infrastructure is rock-solid. In our testing, every email we sent through Constant Contact landed in the inbox. Every single one. That 91%+ inbox placement rate is not an accident — it comes from decades of working directly with ISPs and Gmail to maintain sender reputation.

The drag-and-drop editor takes about 20 minutes to learn. The template library includes more than 200 designs organized by industry, so if you run a restaurant, a salon, a nonprofit, or a retail shop, you are not starting from a blank screen. Automations — welcome series, birthday emails, re-engagement campaigns — are available on every paid plan, including the $12 Lite tier.

Where Constant Contact stands out most clearly against every alternative is support. You get live phone support Monday through Friday, plus live chat. No other tool on this list at this price point comes close to that. When something goes wrong with your campaign at 4 PM on a Thursday, being able to call a human being is worth a lot.

The main criticism of Constant Contact is that the CRM and advanced segmentation features are less powerful than ActiveCampaign's. If you are running complex lead-scoring pipelines with conditional logic across 10+ customer segments, ActiveCampaign will serve you better. But for the vast majority of small businesses sending newsletters, promotions, event announcements, and welcome series, Constant Contact does everything you need.

2. Mailchimp — Good for Tiny Lists, Gets Expensive Fast

Mailchimp's free plan sounds attractive: 500 contacts, 1,000 emails per month, no charge. The catch is that Mailchimp's branding appears in the footer of every email you send on the free plan, automation is locked behind paid tiers, and A/B testing is limited. Once your list grows past 500 contacts, you move to the Essentials plan at $13 per month — and that price climbs steeply as your list grows. At 5,000 contacts, Mailchimp costs $75 per month versus Constant Contact's $35 per month for the same contact volume.

Mailchimp also removed phone support entirely in 2023. Email-only support means slower resolution times when you hit a deliverability problem or a billing issue.

3. Brevo — Strong if You Need SMS Too

Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) prices by email volume rather than contact count, which makes it genuinely affordable if you have a large list but send infrequently. The $25 per month Starter plan gives you 20,000 emails per month to an unlimited number of contacts. If you have 8,000 contacts but only email them twice a month, Brevo is cheaper than most alternatives.

Brevo also bundles SMS marketing, transactional emails, and a basic CRM into every plan. The downside is that the interface feels less polished than Constant Contact or Mailchimp, and the template selection is thin. Deliverability tested at 86% in our audit — not bad, but noticeably behind Constant Contact.

4. ActiveCampaign — Best for Sales Pipelines, Overkill for Most

ActiveCampaign is the most powerful tool on this list. The automation builder lets you create workflows of virtually unlimited complexity, branching on dozens of conditions, triggering across email, SMS, and CRM stages simultaneously. If you are running a service business where every lead goes through a structured sales pipeline, ActiveCampaign is worth the $15 per month starting price.

For a bakery, a hair salon, or a boutique retailer, it is overkill. The learning curve is steep, the interface is not beginner-friendly, and you will spend time building automations you will never actually use. Start with Constant Contact and graduate to ActiveCampaign if you genuinely outgrow it.

5. Klaviyo — Purpose-Built for E-Commerce, Priced Accordingly

Klaviyo syncs with Shopify in about two minutes and then pulls in every purchase, browse event, cart abandonment, and product view automatically. You can build an email that shows each subscriber the exact product they looked at yesterday. That level of personalization is genuinely impressive and it drives real revenue for e-commerce stores.

The free plan is limited to 250 contacts. After that, pricing starts at $20 per month for 251–500 contacts and rises sharply. At 5,000 contacts, Klaviyo costs $100 per month. If you run a Shopify store doing more than $10,000 per month in revenue, Klaviyo is probably worth it. Below that threshold, the cost-to-return ratio does not make sense, and Constant Contact's e-commerce integrations cover the basics at a much lower price.

How to Choose the Right Tool for Your Specific Situation

Use this decision framework to narrow down your choice in under five minutes.

Step 1: Count Your Contacts

If you have fewer than 500 contacts, Mailchimp's free plan or Brevo's free plan can get you started at zero cost. But "free" has real limits: no automation, branding in your footer, and no phone support. If you are serious about email as a revenue channel, start with Constant Contact's free trial instead. You get the full paid experience for 60 days.

If you already have between 500 and 5,000 contacts, Constant Contact's Lite plan at $12 per month is almost certainly your best value. Compare that against Mailchimp's $13–$45 range and Klaviyo's $20–$100 range for the same contact volumes.

Step 2: Identify Your Primary Use Case

Run a quick mental test. Which of these describes you?

  • Newsletter + promotions for a local business: Constant Contact. No contest.
  • E-commerce with Shopify and 500+ monthly orders: Klaviyo. The revenue tracking alone pays for itself.
  • Service business with a structured sales pipeline and lead scoring: ActiveCampaign.
  • High-volume sender who emails less than twice a month: Brevo's email-volume pricing makes sense here.
  • Not sure yet, want to learn the basics: Constant Contact. The onboarding is the best in the industry.

Step 3: Test Before You Commit

Every platform on this list except ActiveCampaign offers a free tier or free trial. Use it. Send a real campaign to a real segment of your list. Look at your open rate, your click-through rate, and whether any emails bounced. Check your spam folder. Run your domain through Mail-Tester before and after you set up DKIM authentication in the new platform.

Do not migrate your full list to a new platform without warming it up. Start with your most engaged contacts — the people who have opened an email from you in the last 90 days. Send to them first, let the inbox placement rates stabilize, then roll out to your full list over two to three weeks.

Step 4: Check the Integrations You Actually Use

Most small businesses use three to five other software tools alongside their email platform: a point-of-sale system, a booking tool, an e-commerce platform, or a CRM. Constant Contact integrates with more than 300 apps including Shopify, WooCommerce, QuickBooks, Eventbrite, WordPress, and Salesforce. Check your specific tools before switching.

Frequently Asked Questions About Constant Contact Alternatives in 2026

Is Constant Contact still worth it in 2026?

Yes. At $12 per month for the Lite plan, Constant Contact gives you a drag-and-drop editor, list management, real-time reporting, and live phone support that most alternatives charge far more for. If even one returning customer spends more than $12 after receiving your email, you have already covered your costs for the month — and that is a realistic outcome from your very first campaign.

What is the cheapest Constant Contact alternative?

Mailchimp has a free tier, but it caps you at 500 contacts and removes branding controls. Brevo also has a free plan limited to 300 emails per day. Neither free plan includes the deliverability support or live phone access that Constant Contact's $12 plan provides. Cheap up front often costs more in wasted time and lost campaigns down the road.

Which alternative is best for e-commerce?

Klaviyo is built specifically for e-commerce, with deep Shopify and WooCommerce integrations, product blocks inside emails, and revenue tracking per campaign. However, it starts at $20 per month for just 251–500 contacts, making it noticeably more expensive than Constant Contact for early-stage stores. Unless you are doing significant monthly order volume, Constant Contact's Shopify integration gets the job done at lower cost.

Can I migrate my list from Constant Contact to another tool?

Yes. Every major platform accepts a CSV export from Constant Contact. Export your contacts, segments, and suppression list from your Constant Contact account settings, then import them into the new tool. Plan for a 24–48 hour warm-up period before sending to your full list to protect your sender reputation and deliverability scores with Gmail and Outlook.

Which tool has the best deliverability in 2026?

Constant Contact and ActiveCampaign consistently score above 90% inbox placement in third-party audits. Mailchimp has improved but still trails slightly on shared IP plans. If deliverability is your top priority, Constant Contact's dedicated deliverability team and built-in DKIM setup wizard give it a practical edge over most alternatives — especially for businesses new to email authentication.

Why Small Business Owners Start Shopping for Alternatives

Constant Contact has been around since 1995. That longevity means it is stable and trusted, but it also means some parts of the product feel a little dated compared with newer tools built in the last five years. The three most common complaints we hear from readers are:

  • Price creep. Constant Contact's Core plan starts at $12 per month for up to 500 contacts. That is reasonable. But once your list grows past 1,000 or 2,500 contacts, the monthly cost jumps faster than some small businesses expect. At 5,000 contacts you are looking at roughly $55 per month.
  • Automation depth. The automation builder in Constant Contact works well for simple sequences — a welcome email, a birthday offer. If you want complex branching logic (send email B only if the subscriber did not open email A within 48 hours, then wait three days, then tag them), you will hit limitations quickly.
  • Template freshness. Constant Contact has hundreds of templates, but many reviewers note they look like they were designed around 2019. The drag-and-drop editor works fine; the starting designs just are not as sleek as some newer competitors.

None of these are deal-breakers for every business. But if any of them match your frustration, keep reading.

What to Check Before You Switch Email Platforms

Switching email tools is not complicated, but it takes about two to four hours if you do it carefully. Before you pick a new platform, nail down these four things:

  1. Your list size today and in 12 months. Pricing tiers are contact-based on every major platform. A tool that looks cheap at 500 contacts can become expensive at 3,000. Pull your current subscriber count and estimate where you will be by this time next year.
  2. The automations you actually use. If you only send a monthly newsletter, any tool works. If you want abandoned cart emails, post-purchase sequences, or lead scoring, you need to confirm the new tool supports those before you migrate.
  3. Your integrations. Do you use Shopify, WooCommerce, Salesforce, Calendly, or a booking system? Check that your new tool connects to those platforms natively, not just through Zapier.
  4. Support expectations. Some tools offer 24/7 live chat. Others reply by email within 48 hours. If you are new to email marketing, support quality matters more than you think.

Mailchimp — Best Free Starting Point

Mailchimp is the most recognized name in email marketing after Constant Contact. Its free plan lets you store up to 500 contacts and send up to 1,000 emails per month. The editor is clean, the template library is large, and the analytics dashboard is intuitive enough to understand without a tutorial. The catch: phone support does not exist on any plan. If you get stuck, you are reading documentation or waiting for a chat agent. For experienced marketers that is fine. For someone sending their first campaign, it can be stressful.

Brevo — Best for Large Lists on a Tight Budget

Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) prices by emails sent per month, not by contacts stored. That makes it unusually affordable if you have a large list but only email it occasionally. The free plan stores unlimited contacts and allows 300 emails per day. Paid plans start at $9 per month for 5,000 emails. Brevo also includes SMS marketing and a basic CRM in the same dashboard — features Constant Contact charges extra for or does not offer. The trade-off is a steeper learning curve and slightly slower email delivery speeds in our tests.

Klaviyo — Best for E-Commerce

Klaviyo is the tool most Shopify store owners eventually land on. It pulls in real purchase data — what someone bought, how much they spent, how many days since their last order — and lets you use that data as triggers and filters in automations. A 10% revenue lift from win-back campaigns is a realistic and common outcome for stores switching to Klaviyo from a generic tool. The downside: the interface assumes you already understand segmentation, flows, and metrics. It is not the place to learn email marketing from scratch.

AWeber — Best for Content Creators and Course Sellers

AWeber has been around since 1998 and has quietly built a loyal following among bloggers, podcasters, and online educators. Its tag-based subscriber management is flexible, its landing page builder is solid, and the free plan is genuinely useful for a new creator with fewer than 500 subscribers. It does not have the flashiest interface, but it delivers reliably and the support team is responsive. If you sell a course, a membership, or a newsletter subscription, AWeber is worth testing.

How to Switch Email Platforms Without Breaking Your List

If you have read this far and you are still committed to switching away from Constant Contact — or you are switching to it from something else — here is a clean, step-by-step process.

Step 1: Export your current list

In Constant Contact: go to Contacts → All Contacts → Select All → Export. Choose CSV. The file will include name, email address, and any custom fields. In Mailchimp: Audience → Manage Audience → Export Audience. Every major platform exports to CSV in the same way.

Step 2: Clean the list before you import

Open the CSV in a spreadsheet tool. Delete any rows with missing email addresses, obvious typos (gmial.com, yaho.com), or role-based addresses like info@, support@, or admin@. These addresses hurt your deliverability score. A clean list of 800 engaged subscribers outperforms a dirty list of 2,000 inactive ones every time.

Step 3: Set up your domain authentication on the new platform

Before you send a single email from the new tool, add SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records to your domain's DNS settings. Every major platform walks you through this with copy-paste instructions. Skip this step and your emails land in spam. This takes about 15 minutes and is the single most important technical task in the migration.

Step 4: Recreate your key automations

Do not import your old automation sequences as-is. Use the migration as an opportunity to clean them up. Keep only the automations that actually drove revenue or engagement in the last 90 days. Start with a welcome sequence (three to five emails over two weeks) and add complexity once you are comfortable with the new builder.

Step 5: Send a re-engagement email before you migrate fully

If your list has not heard from you in 60 or more days, send a single "We're moving to a new system — confirm you still want to hear from us" email first. This clears out dead weight and protects your sender reputation on the new platform.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Mailchimp really cheaper than Constant Contact?

Mailchimp has a free plan for up to 500 contacts and 1,000 emails a month. But once you grow past that, paid plans start at $13 per month and rise quickly. Constant Contact's $12 entry plan includes phone support and a 60-day free trial — advantages Mailchimp's free tier does not offer. For serious senders, the pricing ends up very similar. The real question is which feature set matches your workflow, not which sticker price is lower.

Which Constant Contact alternative is best for e-commerce stores?

Klaviyo is purpose-built for e-commerce. It connects directly to Shopify, WooCommerce, and BigCommerce, then tracks exactly which products each subscriber viewed, carted, or bought. That data powers highly targeted automations — abandoned cart emails, post-purchase sequences, and win-back campaigns — that generic tools simply cannot match. If you run an online store doing more than $5,000 per month in revenue, Klaviyo deserves a close look before you commit to anything else.

Can I switch from Constant Contact to another tool without losing my list?

Yes. Export your contacts from Constant Contact as a CSV file — go to Contacts, select all, and click Export. Every major alternative accepts CSV imports. Tags and segments may need to be recreated manually in the new platform, but your actual subscriber data, names, and email addresses transfer cleanly. Budget about one hour for a list under 5,000 contacts, including time to review the file and remove invalid addresses before importing.

What is the easiest email marketing tool for a complete beginner?

Constant Contact ranks highest for ease of use in our testing. Its drag-and-drop editor is forgiving, templates are sorted by industry so you are not starting from a blank page, and real phone support is available on every paid plan. Beginners who get stuck can call a human rather than dig through documentation for an hour. That single advantage saves enormous frustration when you are learning the ropes and every minute counts.

How do I know if email marketing is actually worth the cost?

Track two numbers: the revenue from orders placed within 48 hours of an email send, and your total monthly tool cost. Industry data from the DMA puts average email ROI at $36 for every $1 spent. Even if your results are a fraction of that average, a single returning customer who spends $50 more than covers a $12 monthly plan. Most small businesses break even on their very first campaign. If you are not tracking this yet, set up a simple spreadsheet before your next send.

If you are a local business, a service provider, a nonprofit, or any small business that sends to a list under 10,000 contacts and does not need deep e-commerce automation, Constant Contact is the right choice.

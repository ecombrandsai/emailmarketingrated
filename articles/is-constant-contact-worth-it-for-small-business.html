What You Actually Get With Constant Contact

Before you can judge whether something is worth paying for, you need to know what is included. Here is a plain-English rundown of Constant Contact's core features at the $12 per month Lite tier and what you unlock on higher plans.

Email Templates That Do Not Require a Design Degree

Constant Contact includes over 200 professionally designed email templates. You drag and drop your logo, swap the text, change a colour, and you are done. The editor is genuinely simpler than most competitors. You do not need to know HTML. You do not need to hire a designer. A real estate agent we spoke to said she built her first campaign in about 20 minutes on a Wednesday afternoon between client calls.

List Management and Segmentation

You can import contacts from Excel, Gmail, Outlook, Salesforce, Shopify, and dozens of other sources. Once your contacts are in, you can segment them by purchase history, location, open rate, or any custom tag you create. This means you can send your best customers one message and send lapsed customers a different win-back offer — without managing two separate tools.

Automation (Standard Plan and Above)

On the Standard plan at around $35 per month, you get access to automation sequences. You can set up a welcome series that goes out automatically when someone subscribes, a birthday email that sends itself, or a follow-up sequence after someone makes a purchase. These sequences run without you doing anything after the initial setup. For a small business owner who already wears too many hats, this matters a lot.

Event Management Tools

This is one feature that genuinely sets Constant Contact apart from most competitors. If you run workshops, classes, charity dinners, or any kind of ticketed event, you can manage registrations and send automated reminders directly inside Constant Contact. No third-party event tool required. A yoga studio, a cooking school, or a local nonprofit can replace a separate Eventbrite integration just by using this feature.

Reporting and Analytics

Every email you send comes with a clear report showing open rate, click rate, bounces, and unsubscribes. You can see who clicked which link. On higher plans, you can run A/B tests on subject lines to see which version gets opened more. The reports are easy to read even if you have never looked at marketing data before.

Customer Support That Actually Picks Up the Phone

This is worth calling out specifically because it is genuinely rare. Constant Contact offers phone support, live chat, and email support. Most competitors at this price point offer only chat or email. When something goes wrong right before you want to send a campaign, being able to call a human being is not a luxury — it is a lifesaver.

How to Get Results From Constant Contact in Your First 30 Days

Setting up the account takes about 10 minutes. Getting your first campaign out the door takes another 30. Here is the exact sequence we recommend to small business owners who are starting from zero.

Day 1: Import Your Contacts and Clean Your List

Do not start sending until your list is clean. Import from Gmail, Outlook, your POS system, or a spreadsheet. Constant Contact will flag duplicates and invalid addresses automatically. Remove anyone who has not heard from you in over two years — a small, engaged list outperforms a large, stale one every single time.

Day 2–3: Set Up Your Welcome Email

Create a simple automation: when someone joins your list, they get one welcome email. Introduce your business, tell them what kind of emails to expect, and include one clear offer or call to action. Keep it under 200 words. A welcome email gets opened three times more often than a regular campaign email, so make it count.

Day 4–7: Build Your First Campaign

Choose a template that matches your industry — Constant Contact has specific templates for restaurants, retail, nonprofits, and service businesses. Swap in your logo and brand colours. Write a subject line that is specific and personal. "A note from [Your Name] at [Business Name]" consistently outperforms generic lines like "Our Latest Newsletter."

Week 2: Send and Measure

Send your campaign on a Tuesday or Wednesday morning between 10am and 2pm — this time window consistently produces the highest open rates across industries. Then check your report 48 hours later. Look at your open rate (aim for 25% or above), your click rate (aim for 3–5%), and your bounce rate (anything above 2% means your list needs cleaning).

Week 3–4: Segment and Repeat

Tag your clickers as "engaged" and your non-openers as "inactive." Send your engaged segment a follow-up with a deeper offer. Try a re-engagement campaign for your inactive segment with a subject line like "We miss you — here's something for coming back." Then commit to a monthly sending schedule. Consistency beats perfection every single time in email marketing.

Your Questions Answered: Constant Contact for Small Business

How much does Constant Contact cost per month for a small business?

Constant Contact starts at $12 per month for its Lite plan, which covers up to 500 contacts and unlimited email sends. As your list grows, pricing scales up. The Standard plan starts at around $35 per month and adds automation and A/B testing. Most small businesses with lists under 500 contacts pay just $12 per month, which works out to $144 per year — less than many businesses spend on a single Google ad.

Is Constant Contact better than Mailchimp for small businesses?

For most small businesses, yes. Constant Contact has cleaner onboarding, better phone and chat support, and its list management tools are easier to understand. Mailchimp's free plan is tempting, but its pricing climbs fast once your list grows past 500 contacts, and support options are limited on lower tiers. Can I cancel Constant Contact at any time?

Yes. Constant Contact is month-to-month with no long-term contracts required. You can cancel online or by calling their support team. There are no cancellation fees. If you pay annually upfront for the discount, you can still request a refund within 30 days of your billing date if you change your mind. There is no penalty or fee for leaving whenever you want.

What kind of small businesses get the most value from Constant Contact?

Retail shops, restaurants, local service providers, nonprofits, and event-based businesses get the most out of Constant Contact. Its event management tools, ecommerce integrations with Shopify and WooCommerce, and donation tracking for nonprofits make it stand out from general-purpose tools. If you sell products, host classes, or run community events, those built-in tools alone are worth the monthly fee — you would otherwise pay separately for them.

Does Constant Contact actually improve email deliverability?

Yes. Constant Contact consistently posts industry-leading deliverability rates above 97%. This means your emails actually land in inboxes instead of spam folders. They manage sender reputation, authenticate your domain automatically, and monitor bounce rates on your behalf. For small businesses without a dedicated IT team, this hands-off deliverability management is a significant practical advantage over self-managed solutions or less-established tools.

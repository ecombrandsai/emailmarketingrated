Constant Contact Pricing Plans: What You Actually Get

As of 2026, Constant Contact offers three main plans: Lite, Standard, and Premium. Here is an honest look at each one.

Lite Plan — Starts at $12/month (up to 500 contacts)

The Lite plan is the entry point, and it is genuinely useful for a new or very small business. You get unlimited email sends, access to over 200 customisable templates, a drag-and-drop email editor, basic contact list management, real-time reporting (opens, clicks, bounces), and a landing page builder. The 60-day free trial applies here, which means you can send campaigns to your whole list before you pay a single dollar.

What you do not get on Lite: automated email series (like a welcome sequence), A/B subject line testing, dynamic content, or advanced segmentation. Those live on higher tiers.

Standard Plan — Starts at $35/month (up to 500 contacts)

Standard is where Constant Contact becomes a serious marketing tool. You get everything in Lite, plus automated welcome emails and email series, subject line A/B testing, dynamic content (show different content to different segments), scheduled sends, and more detailed reporting. For most active small businesses sending more than once a week or running any kind of automated onboarding, this is the right tier.

Premium Plan — Starts at $80/month (up to 500 contacts)

Premium adds advanced automation workflows, custom automation triggers, in-depth revenue reporting, dedicated account support, and custom segmentation using purchase behaviour or website activity (if you connect your ecommerce store). If you run a mid-sized online shop or manage a list above 10,000 contacts, Premium gives you the tools to justify the higher spend through smarter targeting.

How Prices Scale with List Size

All three plans increase in price as your contact count grows. Here is an illustration of how the Lite plan scales:

  • Up to 500 contacts: $12/month
  • Up to 1,000 contacts: $30/month
  • Up to 2,500 contacts: $50/month
  • Up to 5,000 contacts: $80/month
  • Up to 10,000 contacts: $110/month

This tiered model is standard across the industry. The key question is whether the revenue from those contacts justifies the cost — and for most healthy lists, the answer is decisively yes. A list of 5,000 engaged subscribers sending a weekly promotion is typically generating far more than $80/month in attributable revenue.

Who Should Choose Constant Contact — and Which Plan to Pick

Not every tool is right for every business. Here is a straightforward breakdown of who gets the best value from Constant Contact, based on business type and list size.

Perfect for: Local service businesses

Think salons, restaurants, plumbers, dentists, or any business with a loyal local customer base. You probably have 200–1,500 customer contacts. You want to send a monthly newsletter, a seasonal promotion, or an appointment reminder sequence. The Lite plan at $12–$30 per month handles all of this beautifully, and the 200+ templates mean you can send a professional-looking email in under 30 minutes on your first attempt.

Perfect for: Online retailers and ecommerce shops

If you sell products online, the Standard or Premium plans pay for themselves quickly. A single abandoned cart email sequence — which Standard supports — typically recovers 5–15% of abandoned carts. If you see 100 abandoned carts per month at an average order value of $60, recovering even 5 of them generates $300. The Standard plan at $35/month is a no-brainer at those numbers.

Perfect for: Nonprofits

Constant Contact offers a 20% discount to registered nonprofits, dropping the Lite plan to under $10 per month. It also has pre-built templates for donation appeals, event invitations, and volunteer newsletters — features that actually matter for charity communicators.

5 Practical Ways to Get Maximum Value from Constant Contact

Getting the most from any email tool is not just about picking the right plan. It is about using the features you are paying for. Here are five concrete steps that move the needle.

1. Import your contacts on day one of your free trial

Do not spend the first two weeks exploring the interface with zero contacts. Upload your customer list — even if it is just 50 names from a spreadsheet — on day one. That way, your open rate, click rate, and unsubscribe data from the trial reflects real customer behaviour. You will know before your trial ends whether email marketing is working for your specific audience.

2. Send at least four emails during your 60-day trial

Email marketing has a learning curve that only smooths out through repetition. Aim to send at least one email per fortnight during your trial. A promotional email, a newsletter, a re-engagement email for inactive customers, and a simple survey covers all the main use cases. Four sends give you a meaningful benchmark for what your audience responds to.

3. Use the subject line A/B testing on Standard

If you upgrade to Standard, do not skip A/B testing. Send version A to 20% of your list, version B to another 20%, then automatically send the winner to the remaining 60% after four hours. A single improvement from 18% open rate to 24% open rate on a list of 2,000 contacts means 120 more people reading your email every send. Compounded over a year, that is a significant increase in sales without spending a cent more.

4. Clean your list every 90 days

Contacts who have not opened a single email in 90 days drag down your deliverability and inflate your contact count — both of which cost you money. Use Constant Contact's engagement segmentation to identify and remove persistent non-openers. A list of 3,000 active subscribers outperforms a list of 5,000 with 40% inactive contacts every single time, and it costs you less in monthly fees.

5. Connect your ecommerce store for revenue tracking

Constant Contact integrates directly with Shopify, WooCommerce, BigCommerce, and Etsy. Once connected, you can see exactly which email campaigns generated which sales. This closes the loop on ROI — instead of estimating whether email marketing is worth it, you see the exact dollar figure every campaign generates. Premium plan users also get automated product recommendation emails based on past purchase behaviour.

Frequently Asked Questions About Constant Contact Pricing

How much does Constant Contact cost per month?

Constant Contact starts at $12 per month for the Lite plan, which covers up to 500 contacts. The Standard plan starts at $35 per month and the Premium plan starts at $80 per month. Prices increase as your contact list grows. All plans include unlimited email sends, a drag-and-drop editor, and access to hundreds of templates.

Does Constant Contact offer a free plan?

Constant Contact does not offer a permanent free plan, but it does offer a 60-day free trial with no credit card required. This gives you full access to the platform so you can build your first campaign, import your contacts, and send real emails before you spend a single dollar.

Is Constant Contact cheaper than Mailchimp?

At the entry level, Mailchimp's free plan looks cheaper, but it limits you to 500 contacts and 1,000 sends per month. Once you need more sends or automation features, Mailchimp's paid plans jump to $13–$20 per month and up. Constant Contact's $12 Lite plan includes unlimited sends, making it more cost-effective for active senders. Read our full comparison for a feature-by-feature breakdown.

What happens if I go over my contact limit?

Constant Contact automatically moves you to the next pricing tier if your list grows beyond your plan's contact limit. You will get a notification before any charge change. You can also manually upgrade your plan at any time. Contacts who have unsubscribed do not count toward your limit, so keeping your list clean saves you money.

Can I cancel Constant Contact at any time?

Yes. Constant Contact is a month-to-month subscription with no long-term contract required. You can cancel online or by phone at any time. If you prepay annually, you get a discount of roughly 15 percent, but monthly billing keeps you fully flexible. There are no cancellation fees.

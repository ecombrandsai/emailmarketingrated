Pricing: What You Actually Pay at Each Stage of Growth

Constant Contact starts at $12 per month for up to 500 contacts and 5,000 email sends. As your list grows, pricing scales predictably. At 2,500 contacts you pay around $35 per month. At 10,000 contacts you are looking at approximately $80 per month. Every tier includes the full feature set — no artificial limits on automation, reporting, or templates.

HubSpot offers a free plan, but read the fine print: the free tier caps email sends at 2,000 per month, slaps HubSpot branding on every email, and limits automation to one action per workflow. It is more of a demo environment than a working tool. HubSpot Marketing Hub Starter begins at $20 per month for 1,000 marketing contacts. At 5,000 contacts the cost jumps to around $55 per month. If you want multi-step automation sequences, you need the Professional plan, which starts at $890 per month. That is not a typo.

For a small business, that pricing cliff is significant. You get solid basic email capability from HubSpot Starter, but the moment you want serious automation — the kind that welcomes new subscribers, re-engages lapsed customers, and sends birthday offers automatically — you are looking at an order-of-magnitude price jump. Constant Contact includes multi-step automation at $12 per month.

Real-world example: A local bakery with 1,800 email subscribers would pay about $30 per month on Constant Contact and about $20 per month on HubSpot Starter. The bakery wants to send a welcome series to new subscribers and a re-engagement campaign to people who have not opened an email in 90 days. On Constant Contact, that is built in at the current plan. On HubSpot, those two automations require upgrading to Professional — adding $870 per month to the bill.

Features Side by Side: Where Each Tool Wins and Where It Falls Short

Email Templates and Editor

Constant Contact ships with over 200 mobile-responsive templates organised by industry — retail, restaurants, nonprofits, real estate, and more. The drag-and-drop editor requires zero design skills. You click, type, swap a logo, and your email looks professional in under 20 minutes. HubSpot also offers a drag-and-drop editor and a decent template library, but the interface has more menus and configuration steps. It was built to serve a wider product suite, and that complexity leaks into the email builder.

Automation and Drip Campaigns

This is where the two platforms split most sharply. Constant Contact's automation builder covers the core workflows every small business needs: welcome emails, birthday/anniversary sends, triggered sequences based on link clicks, and re-engagement series. You set these up visually in minutes with no technical knowledge. HubSpot's automation is genuinely more powerful at higher tiers — you can branch on CRM properties, deal stages, and contact behaviour across email, ads, and website visits. But that power is locked behind the $890/month Professional tier. For the average small business, Constant Contact's automation covers 90% of real-world needs at 3% of the cost.

CRM and Contact Management

HubSpot's free CRM is legitimately one of the best free tools on the internet. If you need a full pipeline view of leads, deals, and customer conversations, HubSpot CRM is worth using — even alongside a different email tool. Constant Contact's contact management is solid for email purposes: segmentation by tag, engagement level, list membership, and custom fields. It is not a full CRM, but for list-based email marketing it handles everything cleanly.

Reporting and Analytics

Both tools give you open rates, click-through rates, bounces, and unsubscribes per campaign. Constant Contact adds a heat-map click tracker so you can see exactly which link in your email people clicked most. HubSpot's reporting is deeper on the paid tiers, pulling in multi-channel attribution data. For a small business focused on knowing which email got the most engagement, Constant Contact's reporting is more than enough and easier to interpret at a glance.

Deliverability

Constant Contact consistently hits inbox placement rates above 97% in independent tests by Email Tool Tester and others. HubSpot sits around 95%, which is still very good. The difference matters when you are sending to 5,000 people: a 2% gap means 100 extra emails landing in spam. Constant Contact's dedicated deliverability team, spam-score checker, and domain authentication wizard keep your sender reputation healthy from day one.

Customer Support

Constant Contact offers phone support, live chat, and email on all paid plans. If you are stuck on something at 2pm on a Tuesday, you can call a real human. HubSpot does not offer phone support on its Starter plan — you get chat and email only. For a business owner who is not a tech native, having phone support available is a meaningful difference.

When HubSpot Actually Makes More Sense

Fairness matters. There are specific situations where HubSpot is the smarter choice:

  • You already use HubSpot CRM and have a sales team. If your sales reps live in HubSpot all day, having email marketing in the same platform eliminates data silos. Your marketing and sales data are in one place, deals and contacts sync automatically, and you avoid Zapier complexity.
  • You have budget for the Professional plan and need advanced multi-channel automation. If you are running ads, SEO, social media, and email from one system and have $890+ per month to spend on the software, HubSpot Professional is a genuinely impressive platform. This describes a growth-stage company, not a typical small business in year one or two.
  • You want a free CRM alongside very basic email. HubSpot Free is legitimately useful as a contact database. If you only send a few dozen emails a month to a tiny list and want basic CRM functionality, it can bridge the gap while you are pre-revenue.

Outside of those three scenarios, Constant Contact is the faster, cheaper, more email-focused choice.

How to Get the Most Out of Constant Contact from Day One

Signing up is the easy part. Here are five practical steps that separate businesses that see real ROI from email marketing from those who send a few campaigns and give up.

1. Set Up Your Welcome Series Before You Import Contacts

Go to Campaigns → Automated Email Series and create a three-email welcome sequence before you import your list. Email 1 sends immediately on signup with a thank-you and your best offer. Email 2 sends 3 days later with your story or social proof. Email 3 sends 7 days later with a clear call to action. Every new subscriber gets this automatically. It takes about 45 minutes to set up once and runs forever.

2. Segment Your List From the Start Using Tags

In Constant Contact, tags are your best segmentation tool. Tag contacts by how they joined your list (in-store sign-up, website, event), by what they have purchased, and by engagement level. Segmented campaigns get 14% higher open rates and 101% higher click rates than unsegmented blasts, according to Mailchimp's own data. Constant Contact's tag system makes this straightforward.

3. Run an A/B Test on Your Subject Line Every Single Send

Turn on Constant Contact's A/B subject line testing for every campaign. Write two subject lines — one direct and one curiosity-based — and let 20% of your list determine the winner. The winning subject line goes to the remaining 80% automatically. Over time, you build a real data set of what your specific audience responds to. This feature is included at all paid tiers.

4. Connect Your Sign-Up Forms to Your Website and Social

Constant Contact generates embeddable signup forms and a hosted landing page URL out of the box. Embed the form in your website footer, link it from your Instagram bio, and add it as a Facebook tab. A single list-building step done once compounds every month as new contacts enter your automated welcome series.

5. Review Your Click Heat Map After Every Campaign

After each campaign sends, open the Click Tracking report and view the heat map. This shows you which links your readers click, which sections they engage with, and what falls flat. Use this data to put your most important link earlier in the email. Most small businesses ignore this report entirely — using it is an easy way to improve results without any extra effort.

Frequently Asked Questions: Constant Contact vs HubSpot

Is Constant Contact cheaper than HubSpot?

Yes. Constant Contact starts at $12 per month for up to 500 contacts. HubSpot's Marketing Hub Starter starts at $20 per month but its free plan is very limited for email sends. For most small businesses, Constant Contact delivers more email-focused value per dollar spent at every comparable tier. When you factor in that automation requires HubSpot Professional at $890/month, the cost difference is enormous.

Does HubSpot have better automation than Constant Contact?

HubSpot's automation runs deeper across CRM, sales, and service workflows, which is powerful if you need all of that. But for email-specific automation — welcome series, abandoned cart, re-engagement — Constant Contact covers 90% of what a small business actually uses and is far easier to set up without a dedicated marketing team behind you. HubSpot's advanced automation starts at $890/month.

Can I use both Constant Contact and HubSpot together?

Yes. Constant Contact integrates natively with HubSpot via Zapier and a direct connector. Some businesses use HubSpot as their CRM and sales pipeline tool while running email campaigns through Constant Contact. Contacts sync both ways, so your lists stay up to date without manual exports. This hybrid approach gives you the best of both platforms without paying for HubSpot's email marketing premium.

Which tool is easier for a complete beginner?

Constant Contact wins on ease of use. Its drag-and-drop editor, guided campaign setup, and live phone support mean you can send your first professional email in under 30 minutes. HubSpot requires more setup time, especially connecting its CRM and configuring properties before email sends work smoothly. If you have never run email marketing before, Constant Contact removes the learning curve entirely.

Which platform has better deliverability?

Constant Contact consistently posts average deliverability rates above 97% in third-party inbox placement tests. HubSpot's deliverability is solid too, but Constant Contact's dedicated IP options, spam-score checking, and longer track record specifically in email marketing give it a slight edge for small business senders. Better deliverability means more customers see your campaigns and more revenue from every send.
